I've begun to rewatch my favorite comedy show, Portlandia (which I've written about previously), and I'm listening to the commentary tracks for Season 1. Now of course I'm aware they go into these sketches with a fairly definite idea of what they're trying to accomplish, but there's certainly plenty of ad-libbing and improvisation going on, and they end up having to cut a lot of the footage down to get the best lines and takes and keep things tight.

I've never done theater or any sort of acting really, but I did perform on stage for years as a Celtic Folk musician which encompassed a fair degree of improv. You could say it was rather akin to Jazz, as opposed to more classical or even certain pop genres that are more spelled out and rigid.

Learning to embrace "the moment" and be quick on your feet, ready to try out a new lick or go with someone else's flow that they might be into at present, is a skill that's served me well in a variety of other creative avenues. Whether I'm approaching a programming project, recording a podcast episode, cutting a new videoâ€”or even writing this very newsletter when in fact I didn't know what topic I would be covering until I started writing it!â€”I'm not afraid to do improv. Sure, it can help to plan ahead, take down lots of notes, and engage in various other sorts of prep work. But there's something to be said about learning to revel in The Blank Slate, to see that willingness to dive headfirst into uncharted creative waters as a benefit, not a curse.

As an exercise, I hereby challenge you to look over your To Do list or maybe even some past ideas left on the back burner, pick a small project that you'd been itching to tackle, and then try jumping into it as fast and loose as possible. Don't overthink it, don't plan ahead much, don't really try to take it all apart inch by inch in order to come up with a "game plan". Just Do It!! Don't let your dreams be dreams! ðŸ˜‰
