In the previous issue of Creator Class, I talked about the importance of Just Do It(tm). Don’t procrastinate, don’t come up with excuses, don’t lean too heavily on what everybody else is doing. Just. Do! It!

But the ability to do anything creative and meaningful is predicated on the emotional stamina and willpower to be physically able to do it. And sometimes…we just don't have it. We don't have the energy, or the confidence, or the right idea. We get overwhelmed. We get beat down. We get all tangled up in our heads.

I recently watched a new series on Hulu called Up Here, a musical romantic comedy which is much better than that sounds. 😄 Besides featuring great music from the talented folks behind hits such as Frozen and WandaVision, as well as delightful performances by the entire cast, Up Here does a remarkable job of visualizing and personifying the "voices in our heads" — the running dialogue many of us constantly struggle against.

You might boil down this internal monologue into one of two themes:

(a) You can't do it (loser)

(b) You should do X (where X is something dumb that really isn't what you want and doesn't fit into your value system at all, but you feel the pressure to go along with it)

Sometimes you get both themes running in parallel, which will really bake your noodle. 😵‍💫

I'm not going to lie: this has been happening to me lately. At first it was a tiny "splinter in my mind" (to quote The Matrix), affecting only one particular aspect of my overall set of creative and lifestyle goals, but then it started to grow into more and more areas. Why does this happen? Honestly, I don't know. It could be a variety of factors. Not enough sleep. Too much rain. Working on tax paperwork. Seeing yet another facile "AI will take over the world!" headline in my newsfeed. 🤮

Regardless of why this happens, the point is that it happens to all of us at one time or another. And like many sorts of problems we face in life, the only way past it is to acknowledge it and be honest about what you're struggling with. And then tell those voices to go pound sand.

“You can't do it, loser.” Well, maybe I can't. But I can still make the attempt and know that I gave it my all.

“You should do X.” Well, maybe I should. But I choose not to. I'm waiting for a better idea to come along which fully aligns with my values and my goals.

Some days you may feel like you're losing this fight. At the risk of sounding extremely corny, never give up! Never surrender! 😉

Your future self will thank you. Now go get some sleep. 😴
